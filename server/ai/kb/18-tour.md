Guided Tour — How the Walkthrough Works

The portal includes an interactive guided tour that walks new users through every section of the application. Users are offered the tour automatically when they first log in, and it can also be started anytime from the Help page under the Guided Tour tab.

The tour highlights each navigation item in the sidebar and explains what it does. Here is the full sequence:

Step 1 — Dashboard (Home)
This is your home base. It shows a high-level overview of your entire portfolio — key metrics, charts, and recent activity at a glance. The dashboard shows consolidated KPIs including IRR, equity multiple, cash-on-cash return, and total exit value. It also includes income statement, cash flow, and balance sheet summary charts.

Step 2 — Portfolio (Define Your Properties)
Start here when setting up. Add each property you want to model, then fill in the assumptions for each one — purchase price, room count, ADR, occupancy, expenses, and financing terms. This is the foundation of your entire simulation. The portfolio page includes a map view and a list view of all properties.

Step 3 — Management Company
Next, define the management company assumptions — staffing tiers, partner compensation, base and incentive fee structures, and funding instruments. The management company earns fees from the properties you just set up. You can see the company P&L here showing fee revenue minus operating costs.

Step 4 — Systemwide Assumptions (Settings)
Review and adjust the global settings that apply across all properties — tax rates, global inflation default, depreciation schedules, and other defaults. These ensure consistency across your entire portfolio, though specific properties and the management company can override their own inflation rates. Settings include the discount rate, projection period, fiscal year, SAFE funding terms, and partner compensation schedules.

Step 5 — Scenarios (Save & Compare)
Save your current assumptions as a named scenario so you can come back to it later. Create multiple scenarios to compare different strategies — like varying occupancy ramps or financing structures. Each scenario is a full snapshot of every assumption in the model.

Step 6 — Analysis Tools
Explore what is available in the Analysis section — sensitivity tables, financing comparisons, executive summaries, side-by-side property comparisons, and portfolio timelines. This is where you stress-test your assumptions and see the big picture. The executive summary generates a presentation-ready overview of the portfolio.

Step 7 — Help (User Manual & Checker Manual)
Consult the User Manual for a complete guide to every feature — from how revenue is calculated to how the balance sheet works. There is also a Checker Manual for verifying the financial models. The Help page also has the option to restart the guided tour at any time.

Step 8 — Quick Navigation (Search)
Press Ctrl+K anytime to search and jump to any page, property, or feature instantly. You can also find your favorite properties and recent activity in the sidebar.

Step 9 — Notifications
Check the notification bell for important alerts — like negative cash balance warnings or verification results.

Tour Preferences
Users can choose to skip the tour or check a "Don't offer again" box if they do not want to see the tour prompt on future logins. The preference is saved to their profile. The tour can always be restarted manually from the Help page regardless of this setting.

Recommended Workflow for New Users
The tour suggests a specific order for getting started:
1. Define your properties in the Portfolio section
2. Set up the Management Company assumptions
3. Review Systemwide Settings for consistency
4. Save your first scenario as "Base Case"
5. Use the Analysis tools to stress-test your assumptions
6. Consult the Help section if you need more detail on any calculation
